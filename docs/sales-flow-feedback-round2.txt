Thought for 4s

I agree with Viktor’s second take more than the conservative sprint-only take.

He’s right: if you’re not worried about scope creep, then the full flow is worth doing because the current wizard has the ingredients, but the order is not maximizing the psychology. The new flow is not just “prettier.” It creates a stronger buying journey: personal relevance → proof → concern → solution → transformation → financial justification → decision. That is the right emotional sequence for in-home water sales.

My honest opinion

The proposed flow is genuinely better.

The conservative version was right if your goal was “fix the app fast.” But your goal sounds more like:

Make this presentation an elite dealer closing system.

If that is the goal, I would absolutely lean into the stronger flow.

The current flow is more like:

Here’s your report → here’s a bunch of data → here’s a test → here’s the system → here’s the price.

The better flow is:

Here’s your home → here’s what we found → let’s verify it live → here’s what it means → here’s how it affects your family/home → here’s the fix → here’s the transformation → here’s why the price makes sense → what do you want to do next?

That is a much stronger psychological arc.

Where Viktor is 100% right
1. Home profile before score is smart

Do not jump straight into the score.

Start with:

“This is your home’s water profile.”

Show:

customer name
address or area
utility provider
city/state
family size if known
current water habits
current concerns

Why it works:

The homeowner starts thinking:

“This is about me.”

That makes the score land harder.

2. Live test early is non-negotiable

This is probably the biggest improvement.

The flow should be:

Local data says this → let’s verify your tap water right now → live test confirms it.

That is psychologically powerful because it removes the “this is generic data” objection.

The live test should not be buried. It should be the moment where the buyer shifts from passive listener to active believer.

3. Cost of inaction before pricing is absolutely correct

This is textbook high-ticket sales.

Never show a big price before establishing what the problem is already costing them.

Before price, show:

bottled water
fridge filters
pitcher filters
water delivery
appliance wear
plumbing buildup
skin/hair products
fixture cleaning
laundry issues
water heater stress

Then the system price becomes a comparison.

Not:

“This costs $9,995.”

But:

“You may already be spending money every month because of poor water. This redirects that money into actually fixing the source.”

That is way stronger.

4. The decision page is a great idea

The current ending with QR handoff feels unfinished.

A final QR code is useful, but it is not a close.

You need a final page that gives the rep a clean decision moment:

Choose Your Next Step

Option 1: Move forward today
Lock in installation and available savings.

Option 2: Schedule a follow-up
Review with spouse or decision-maker.

Option 3: Send my report
Text/email the report and keep the file open.

This is strong because even if they do not buy, they do not just vanish. They move into a follow-up path.

Where I slightly disagree with Viktor
Family Impact and Home Impact should not be squeezed too much

He suggested making Family Impact / Home Impact one screen with tabs. That may work, but I would be careful.

Those are two different emotional triggers.

Family impact creates emotional urgency.
Home impact creates financial justification.

If you combine them too tightly, you may weaken both.

My recommendation:

Use one main section called:

What This Means for Your Home

Then inside it have two large cards or tabs:

Your Family

Drinking, cooking, showers, skin, hair, kids, pets.

Your House

Appliances, plumbing, fixtures, laundry, water heater.

So yes, combine the area visually, but do not make either one feel like a small detail.

Projected Score should be both its own moment and tied to the solution

Viktor said the projected score should be merged into the solution screen. I half-agree.

I would do this:

First, on the solution screen, show:

Recommended System → Projected Score: 94

Then immediately after, have a dramatic transformation screen:

Your Water Score Journey

Starting Score: 30
Verified Score: 55
Projected With System: 94

That score journey is too powerful to hide inside one solution card. It should get its own emotional “wow” moment.

“What matters most to you?” should be built now

I agree with Viktor here.

This is not too hard, and it would make the presentation feel custom immediately.

At the beginning, ask:

What matters most to you?
Better drinking water
Health/family peace of mind
Better showers / skin / hair
Appliance and plumbing protection
Taste and odor
Saving money on bottled water
Stains, buildup, or cleaning issues

Then later, the presentation can highlight that concern.

Even if you do not build full AI personalization yet, you can use this selection to reorder or emphasize sections.

Example:

If they choose appliances, show Home Impact before Family Impact.

If they choose kids/family, show Family Impact first.

If they choose taste/odor, emphasize chlorine and drinking water.

That one question makes the whole thing feel 10x more personal.

The flow I would actually build

Here is my ideal version after combining my original idea, Viktor’s pushback, and what I saw in the demo.

1. Welcome / Agenda

Purpose: lower resistance.

Copy:

Today we’ll walk through your home’s water profile, verify it with a live test, and show what options exist if you decide you want to improve it.

This makes it feel educational, not pushy.

2. Home Water Profile

Purpose: make it personal.

Show:

customer name
area
utility provider
household details
current concerns
water source
current filter setup

Headline:

Your Home Water Profile

Subtext:

This report is based on the water provider serving your home, combined with today’s live in-home test.

3. What Matters Most to You?

Purpose: create personalization and micro-commitment.

Options:

Drinking water
Family health
Skin and hair
Appliances/plumbing
Taste or smell
Stains/buildup
Bottled water costs

This is not just data collection. It makes the customer tell you what emotional angle matters most.

4. Local Water Report

Purpose: establish authority.

Show:

utility name
source
detected contaminants
top concern categories
data source
last updated if possible

Do not lead with the full contaminant table.

Lead with:

Top 3 Concerns Found in Your Area

Then expandable full list.

5. Initial Water Score

Purpose: create concern.

Show score ring.

But include methodology:

Your score is based on local water data, contaminant levels, health guideline comparisons, hardness/mineral levels, disinfectant levels, and live test results when available.

This prevents the score from feeling made up.

6. Live Water Test

Purpose: create belief.

This should happen early.

The rep says:

The report gives us the local picture. Now let’s see what’s actually coming out of your tap.

Test:

chlorine
hardness
TDS
pH
iron/sediment if applicable
7. Verified Score / Test Results

Purpose: connect data to physical proof.

Show:

Local Report Score: 30
Live Test Verified Score: 55

Then explain:

The local report shows what is commonly found in your water system. The live test helps show what is present in the home today.

8. What This Means for Your Family and Home

Purpose: emotional and logical relevance.

Two tabs/cards:

Your Family
drinking
cooking
showers
skin
hair
kids
pets
Your Home
water heater
dishwasher
washing machine
plumbing
fixtures
laundry
scale buildup

This section should adapt based on the “What matters most?” answer.

9. Recommended Solution

Purpose: introduce the fix.

Lead with benefits first:

What this system does:

cleaner water throughout the home
reduces chlorine taste and odor
improves shower comfort
helps protect appliances
reduces hard water buildup
supports better drinking/cooking water

Then expandable technical details:

carbon filtration
softener
sediment filter
smart valve
bypass
install details
10. Score Transformation

Purpose: create the “wow” moment.

This should be a dramatic screen.

Your Water Score Journey

Before: 30
Verified Today: 55
With Recommended System: 94

Visual:

30 → 55 → 94

This is one of the strongest sales moments in the whole wizard.

11. Proof / Trust

Purpose: remove risk.

Show:

reviews
local installs
warranty
certifications
homes protected
company/team photo
service area
support promise

I agree with Viktor: add a small trust badge strip earlier, but keep the full proof section here.

12. Cost of Doing Nothing

Purpose: price anchoring.

Show estimated current hidden costs:

bottled water
filters
water delivery
appliance wear
plumbing buildup
cleaning products
skin/hair product compensation

Headline:

What untreated water may already be costing you

This should come directly before price.

13. Investment

Purpose: show price clearly.

This screen must be flawless.

No broken values. No negative numbers. No weird discount stack.

Show:

Retail Package: $X
Program Savings: -$X
Installed Price: $X
Estimated Monthly: $X/mo

Keep it simple and premium.

14. Financing / Payment Options

Purpose: reduce friction.

Show:

monthly payment
term options
same-as-cash if applicable
no prepayment penalty if true
install deposit if any

The buyer should feel:

“This is manageable.”

15. Decision Page

Purpose: close or capture follow-up.

Options:

Move Forward Today

Schedule installation / lock in savings.

Schedule Follow-Up

Send to spouse or decision-maker.

Send My Report

Text/email report and keep options open.

This is much better than ending with only a QR code.

16. Final Report / QR

Purpose: leave behind proof.

Show:

QR code
text/email report
summary
dealer contact
next step

Bottom line

Viktor is right.

The full flow is not just scope creep. It is the better version of the product.

If you only want a quick fix, do Sprint 5A.

But if you want this to become a serious dealer sales weapon, build the improved flow.

My honest recommendation:

Do the bigger flow, but do it intelligently. Do not rebuild the whole app visually. Reorder the sales journey, add the missing screens, and keep the premium dashboard style.

That gives you the best of both worlds:

better psychology
stronger close
less buyer confusion
higher perceived authority
more emotional momentum
without throwing away what already works
